Research

Our research focuses on developing and applying computational methods to solve problems in chemistry and materials science. We combine quantum chemistry, molecular dynamics, and machine learning to understand chemical phenomena and design new materials.

Computational Chemistry

Quantum Mechanical Calculations

We develop and apply quantum chemical methods to study molecular structures, reaction mechanisms, and electronic properties. Our work includes density functional theory (DFT), coupled cluster methods, and beyond.

Reaction Mechanism Discovery

Using automated computational workflows, we discover and characterize reaction pathways for complex chemical transformations. This work has applications in catalysis, organic synthesis, and atmospheric chemistry.

Machine Learning for Chemistry

Property Prediction

We develop machine learning models to predict molecular and materials properties. Our models enable rapid screening of chemical space and guide experimental efforts.

Neural Network Potentials

We create machine learning potentials that enable molecular dynamics simulations with near-quantum mechanical accuracy at a fraction of the computational cost.

Materials Design

Functional Materials

We design and optimize materials for specific applications including energy storage, catalysis, and electronic devices. Our computational screening methods accelerate materials discovery.

Polymer Science

We study polymer properties and behavior using molecular simulations. This includes work on polymer electrolytes, degradation mechanisms, and structure-property relationships.

Software Development

Open Source Tools

We develop and maintain open-source software for the computational chemistry community. Our tools enable researchers to perform complex calculations and analyze results efficiently.
